Proposition 1.3.17. Cauchy-Schwarz inequality.

If \(\vvec\) and \(\wvec\) are two vectors in an inner product space \(V\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} |\inner{\vvec}{\wvec}| \leq \len{\vvec}\len{\wvec}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Proof.

We first apply the projection formula to find \(\what\text{,}\) the orthogonal projection of \(\wvec\) onto \(\vvec\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \what = \frac{\inner{\wvec}{\vvec}} {\inner{\vvec}{\vvec}} \vvec\text{.} \end{equation*}
We know that \(\what\) and \(\wvec-\what\) are orthogonal by the construction of the orthogonal projection. Therefore, the Pythagorean theorem applies.
\begin{align*} \wvec \amp = \what + (\wvec - \what)\\ \len{\wvec}^2 \amp = \len{\what}^2 + \len{\wvec - \what}^2\\ \len{\wvec}^2 \amp \geq \len{\what}^2 \\ \len{\wvec}^2 \amp \geq \len{\frac{\inner{\wvec}{\vvec}} {\inner{\vvec}{\vvec}}}^2 \len{\vvec}^2\\ \len{\wvec}^2 \amp \geq \frac{\len{\inner{\wvec}{\vvec}}^2} {\len{\vvec}^2}\\ \len{\vvec}^2\len{\wvec}^2 \amp \geq \len{\inner{\wvec}{\vvec}}^2\\ \len{\vvec}\len{\wvec} \amp \geq \len{\inner{\wvec}{\vvec}}\text{.} \end{align*}
