Theorem 1.4.6. Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.

If \(p(x)\) is a degree \(n\) polynomial having complex coefficients, then there are \(n\) roots, \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \ldots, \lambda_n\text{,}\) possibly with repetition. This means that
\begin{equation*} p(x)=a_n(x-\lambda_1)(x-\lambda_2)\ldots(x-\lambda_n)\text{.} \end{equation*}
