Proposition 2.2.7.

If \(V\) is a real inner product space and \(T\) is a self-adjoint operator on \(T\text{,}\) then the minimal polynomial of \(T\) has the form
\begin{equation*} p(x)=(x-\lambda_1)(x-\lambda_2)\ldots(x-\lambda_m) \end{equation*}
where \(\lambda_j\in\real\text{.}\)

Proof.

We know that the minimal polynomial has the form
\begin{equation*} p(x)=(x-\lambda_1)\ldots(x-\lambda_m)(x^2+b_1x+c_1)\ldots (x^2+b_kx+c_k) \end{equation*}
where \(b_i^2 \lt 4c_i\) for each \(i\text{.}\) However, since \(p(T)=0\text{,}\) we know that
\begin{equation*} p(T)=(T-\lambda_1)\ldots(T-\lambda_m)(T^2+b_1T+c_1I)\ldots (T^2+b_kT+c_kI)\text{.} \end{equation*}
If \(k\gt 0\text{,}\) then \(T^2+b_1T+c_1I\) is invertible by LemmaÂ 2.2.6. If we multiply \(p(T)\) by its inverse, we obtain another polynomial \(q\) of smaller degree for which \(q(T)=0\text{.}\)
